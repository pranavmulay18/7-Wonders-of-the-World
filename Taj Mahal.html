TAJ MAHAL

Introduction

The Taj Mahal is the( 'Crown of the Palace') is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India

It was commissioned in 1631 by the fifth Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himself

The tomb is the centrepiece of a 17-hectare (42-acre) complex, which includes a mosque and a guest house, and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a crenellated wall.

Inspiration

he Taj Mahal was commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1631, to be built in the memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died on 17 June that year, while giving birth to their 14th child, Gauhara Begum.[14][15] Construction started in 1632, and the mausoleum was completed in 1648, while the surrounding buildings and garden were finished five years later.

Architecture and design

The Taj Mahal incorporates and expands on design traditions of Indo-Islamic and earlier Mughal architecture. Specific inspiration came from successful Timurid and Mughal buildings including the Gur-e Amir (the tomb of Timur, progenitor of the Mughal dynasty, in Samarkand), Humayun's Tomb which inspired the Charbagh gardens and hasht-behesht (architecture) plan of the site, Itmad-Ud-Daulah's Tomb , and Shah Jahan's own Jama Masjid in Delhi. While earlier Mughal buildings were primarily constructed of red sandstone, Shah Jahan promoted the use of white marble inlaid with semi-precious stones. Buildings under his patronage reached new levels of refinement.

DESIGN

Tomb

The tomb is the central focus of the entire complex of the Taj Mahal. It is a large, white marble structure standing on a square plinth and consists of a symmetrical building with an iwan (an arch-shaped doorway) topped by a large dome and finial. Like most Mughal tombs, the basic elements are Indo-Islamic in origin.

Exterior decorations

The exterior decorations of the Taj Mahal are among the finest in Mughal architecture. As the surface area changes, the decorations are refined proportionally.

Interior decoration

The interior chamber of the Taj Mahal reaches far beyond traditional decorative elements. The inlay work is not pietra dura, but a lapidary of precious and semiprecious gemstones.

Garden

The complex is set around a large 300-metre (980 ft) square charbagh or Mughal garden. The garden uses raised pathways that divide each of the four-quarters of the garden into 16 sunken parterres or flowerbeds.

Outlying buildings

The Taj Mahal complex is bordered on three sides by crenellated red sandstone walls; the side facing the river is open. Outside the walls are several additional mausoleums, including those of Shah Jahan's other wives, and a larger tomb for Mumtaz's favourite servant.[

he main gateway (darwaza) is a monumental structure built primarily of marble, and reminiscent of the Mughal architecture of earlier emperors. Its archways mirror the shape of the tomb's archways, and its pishtaq arches incorporate the calligraphy that decorates the tomb. It uses bas-relief and pietra dura inlaid decorations with floral motifs. The vaulted ceilings and walls have elaborate geometric designs like those found in the other sandstone buildings in the complex

Construction

The Taj Mahal is built on a parcel of land to the south of the walled city of Agra. Shah Jahan presented Maharaja Jai Singh I with a large palace in the centre of Agra in exchange for the land.[46] An area of roughly 1.2 hectares (3 acres) was excavated, filled with dirt to reduce seepage, and levelled at 50 metres (160 ft) above the riverbank level. In the tomb area, piles were dug and filled with stone and rubble to form the footings of the tomb. Instead of lashed bamboo, workmen constructed a colossal brick scaffold that mirrored the tomb. The scaffold was so enormous that foremen expected it to take years to dismantle.

Symbolism

Due to the global attention that it has received and the millions of visitors it attracts, the Taj Mahal has become a prominent image that is associated with India, and in this way has become a symbol of India itself./p>

Later days

Soon after the Taj Mahal's completion, Shah Jahan was deposed by his son Aurangzeb and put under house arrest at the nearby Agra Fort from where he could see the Taj Mahal. Upon Shah Jahan's death, Aurangzeb buried him in the mausoleum next to his wife.[61] In the 18th century, the Jat rulers of Bharatpur invaded Agra and attacked the Taj Mahal. They took away the two chandeliers, one of agate and another of silver, which were hung over the main cenotaph; they also took the gold and silver screen. Kanbo, a Mughal historian, said the gold shield which covered the 4.6-metre-high (15 ft) finial at the top of the main dome was also removed during the Jat despoilation

TOURSIM

The Taj Mahal attracts a large number of tourists. UNESCO documented more than 2 million visitors in 2001, which had increased to about 7–8 million in 2014.

Myths

Ever since its construction, the building has been the source of an admiration transcending culture and geography, and so personal and emotional responses have consistently eclipsed scholastic appraisals of the monument.

Access and Transportation

tAJ MAHAL is accessible via train, bus, or hiking trails such as the famous Inca Trail. Travelers often arrive at the nearby town of Aguas Calientes before taking a bus or hiking up to the citadel. Entry to Machu Picchu is regulated and requires tickets purchased in advance.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May to October) is generally considered the best time to visit Machu Picchu. During this period, the weather is typically clear, offering breathtaking views of the citadel and surrounding landscapes. The wet season (November to April) brings rain and occasional fog, affecting visibility but often resulting in fewer crowds.

Altitude and Acclimatization

TAJ MAHLAL sits at an altitude of approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet). Visitors coming from lower elevations are advised to acclimate themselves to the altitude to prevent altitude sickness. Spending a day or two in Cusco or other high-altitude areas before visiting can help mitigate these effects.

Entry Tickets and Permits

Entry to TAJ MAHAL requires purchasing tickets in advance. Additionally, if planning to hike the Inca Trail or visit Huayna Picchu, separate permits are necessary, and they often need to be booked well ahead of time due to limited availability.

Visitor Guidelines

To preserve the site, visitors are expected to adhere to guidelines such as not littering, not touching the ruins, and staying on designated paths. Professional tour guides are available and recommended for a deeper understanding of the site's history and significance.

Planning a visit to TAJ MAHAL involves considering various factors for a smooth and enriching experience. Whether arriving by train, bus, or hiking through the scenic trails, careful preparation ensures a rewarding exploration of this ancient wonder.

Tips for Tourists

Visiting TAJ MAHAL is a remarkable experience. Here are some valuable tips to enhance your journey:

Plan and Book in Advance

Due to its popularity and limited daily entries, it's crucial to plan and book tickets well in advance. This includes entry tickets to Machu Picchu, permits for hiking trails like the Inca Trail or Huayna Picchu, and accommodations in Aguas Calientes or nearby areas.

Acclimatize to the Altitude

Given TAJ MAHAL elevation, acclimatization is vital. Spend a day or two in Cusco or other high-altitude places before your visit to avoid altitude sickness. Drink plenty of water, take it slow, and heed your body's signals.

Be Mindful of the Weather

Weather in the Andes can be unpredictable. While the dry season (May to October) is ideal for clearer views, the wet season (November to April) brings lush landscapes but also rain and fog. Pack accordingly with rain gear and layers.

Wear Comfortable Footwear and Clothing

Exploring TAJ MAHAL involves walking on uneven terrain and stairs. Comfortable, sturdy footwear is essential. Dress in layers to adapt to changing temperatures, and don't forget a hat, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Respect the Site

TAJ MAHAL is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Respect the rules by not littering, avoiding touching the ruins, and staying on designated paths. Preserve this historic wonder for future generations to enjoy.

Consider a Guided Tour

Professional guides offer insights into TAJ MAHAL history and significance, enriching your visit. They can navigate the site efficiently, providing historical context and hidden details you might miss otherwise.

Bring Essentials and Stay Hydrated

Carry essentials like a reusable water bottle, snacks, a camera, and necessary personal items. Staying hydrated is crucial at higher altitudes, so keep drinking water throughout your visit.

Enjoy the Moment

Amidst the awe-inspiring landscapes and ancient ruins, take moments to pause, absorb the surroundings, and appreciate the significance of Machu Picchu. Embrace the experience and create lasting memories.
