PETRA

Introduction

Petra (Arabic: ٱلْبَتْراء, romanized: Al-Batraʾ; Greek: Πέτρα, "Rock"), originally known to its inhabitants as Raqmu , is a historic and archaeological city in southern Jordan.

Famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system, Petra is also called the "Rose City" because of the colour of the sandstone from which it is carved; it was famously called "a rose-red city half as old as time" in a poem of 1845 by John Burgon.

The tomb is the centrepiece of a 17-hectare (42-acre) complex, which includes a mosque and a guest house, and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a crenellated wall.

History

Bronze Age

Petra is listed in Egyptian campaign accounts and the Amarna letters as Pel, Sela, or Seir

Byzantine period

Petra declined rapidly under Roman rule, in large part from the revision of sea-based trade routes. In 363, an earthquake destroyed many buildings and crippled the vital water management system.

Crusaders and Mamluks

In the 12th century, the Crusaders built fortresses such as the Alwaeira Castle, but were forced to abandon Petra after a while. As a result, the location of Petra was lost until the 19th century.[

21st century

Excavations have demonstrated that it was the ability of the Nabataeans to control the water supply that led to the rise of the desert city, creating an artificial oasis.

Layout

In ancient times, Petra might have been approached from the south on a track leading across the plain of Petra, around Jabal Haroun ("Aaron's Mountain"), the location of the Tomb of Aaron, said to be the burial place of Aaron, brother of Moses.

Hellenistic architecture

Petra is known primarily for its Hellenistic architecture. The facades of the tombs in Petra are commonly rendered in Hellenistic style, reflecting the number of diverse cultures which the Nabataens traded, all of which were in turn influenced by Greek culture. Most of these tombs contain small burials niches carved into the stone

City centre

At the end of the narrow gorge, the Siq, stands Petra's most elaborate ruin, popularly known as Al-Khazneh ("the Treasury"), hewn into the sandstone cliff. While remaining in remarkably preserved condition, the face of the structure is marked by hundreds of bullet holes made by the local Bedouin tribes that hoped to dislodge riches that were once rumoured to be hidden within it.

Myths

Ever since its construction, the building has been the source of an admiration transcending culture and geography, and so personal and emotional responses have consistently eclipsed scholastic appraisals of the monument.

Visiting Information

Visiting PETRA requires careful planning to ensure a memorable and hassle-free experience. Here are essential details to consider before your journey:

Access and Transportation

petra is accessible via train, bus, or hiking trails such as the famous Inca Trail. Travelers often arrive at the nearby town of Aguas Calientes before taking a bus or hiking up to the citadel. Entry to Machu Picchu is regulated and requires tickets purchased in advance.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (May to October) is generally considered the best time to visit Machu Picchu. During this period, the weather is typically clear, offering breathtaking views of the citadel and surrounding landscapes. The wet season (November to April) brings rain and occasional fog, affecting visibility but often resulting in fewer crowds.

Altitude and Acclimatization

petra sits at an altitude of approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet). Visitors coming from lower elevations are advised to acclimate themselves to the altitude to prevent altitude sickness. Spending a day or two in Cusco or other high-altitude areas before visiting can help mitigate these effects.

Entry Tickets and Permits

Entry to petra requires purchasing tickets in advance. Additionally, if planning to hike the Inca Trail or visit Huayna Picchu, separate permits are necessary, and they often need to be booked well ahead of time due to limited availability.

Visitor Guidelines

To preserve the site, visitors are expected to adhere to guidelines such as not littering, not touching the ruins, and staying on designated paths. Professional tour guides are available and recommended for a deeper understanding of the site's history and significance.

Tips for Tourists

Visiting petra is a remarkable experience. Here are some valuable tips to enhance your journey:

Plan and Book in Advance

Due to its popularity and limited daily entries, it's crucial to plan and book tickets well in advance. This includes entry tickets to Machu Picchu, permits for hiking trails like the Inca Trail or Huayna Picchu, and accommodations in Aguas Calientes or nearby areas.

Acclimatize to the Altitude

Given petra elevation, acclimatization is vital. Spend a day or two in Cusco or other high-altitude places before your visit to avoid altitude sickness. Drink plenty of water, take it slow, and heed your body's signals.

Be Mindful of the Weather

Weather in the Andes can be unpredictable. While the dry season (May to October) is ideal for clearer views, the wet season (November to April) brings lush landscapes but also rain and fog. Pack accordingly with rain gear and layers.

Wear Comfortable Footwear and Clothing

Exploring petra involves walking on uneven terrain and stairs. Comfortable, sturdy footwear is essential. Dress in layers to adapt to changing temperatures, and don't forget a hat, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Respect the Site

petra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Respect the rules by not littering, avoiding touching the ruins, and staying on designated paths. Preserve this historic wonder for future generations to enjoy.

Consider a Guided Tour

Professional guides offer insights into TAJ MAHAL history and significance, enriching your visit. They can navigate the site efficiently, providing historical context and hidden details you might miss otherwise.

Bring Essentials and Stay Hydrated

Carry essentials like a reusable water bottle, snacks, a camera, and necessary personal items. Staying hydrated is crucial at higher altitudes, so keep drinking water throughout your visit.

Enjoy the Moment

Amidst the awe-inspiring landscapes and ancient ruins, take moments to pause, absorb the surroundings, and appreciate the significance of Machu Picchu. Embrace the experience and create lasting memories.

Following these tips can make your journey to Petra more enjoyable and fulfilling. Prepare adequately, respect the site, and embrace the wonder of this ancient Incan citadel.
